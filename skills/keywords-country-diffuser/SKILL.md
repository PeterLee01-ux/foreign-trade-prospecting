# Keywords Country Diffuser

Build a sourced, reusable prospect workbook for one target country.

## Workflow

1. Identify the target country, local language, calling code, major regions, and large/medium/small cities.
2. Read [methodology.md](references/methodology.md) before researching or scoring leads.
3. Create English and local-language keyword families:
   - product: scent machine, scent diffuser, cold-air/waterless/nebulizing diffuser, HVAC scent diffuser, commercial scenting system;
   - industry: scent marketing, ambient scenting, commercial scenting, home fragrance, aromatherapy, hotel supplies, air care;
   - buyer: distributor, importer, wholesaler, dealer, supplier, retailer, scent-marketing company, hotel supplier, home-fragrance brand;
   - localized equivalents and spelling variants.
4. Build a Boolean country query using product synonyms + buyer types + country. Treat `two-fluid atomization` as a supporting technical term, not the main customer-search term.
5. Search the public web in English and the country's local language. Prefer official company pages, contact pages, legal notices, reputable B2B directories, and professional associations.
6. Record each prospect with company, match tier, customer type, city/region, address, contact person/role, every public email, direct mobile, every public/company phone, website, source URL, notes, and match score.
7. Retain generic/public emails such as `info@`, `sales@`, `office@`, `contact@`, `bonjour@`, and `commercial@`. Retain switchboards, toll-free numbers, and public hotlines. Mark generic/public emails and public-service/toll-free phone numbers with red fill and red text in Excel. Keep direct personal contacts unmarked.
8. Deduplicate by normalized company/domain/address. If one company has multiple named regional contacts, keep separate rows and explain the relationship.
9. Classify leads:
   - `Direct`: professional scenting, diffuser systems, HVAC scenting, scent marketing;
   - `Channel`: fragrance/home/wellness/hotel/beauty distributors, importers, wholesalers, private-label suppliers;
   - `Adjacent`: fragrance houses, HVAC suppliers, odor-control or related businesses requiring verification.
10. Score 0–100 using the rubric in [methodology.md]. Never invent missing contacts; leave unverified cells blank.
11. Add an `Office Districts` sheet covering representative large, medium, and small cities with business districts, office corridors/streets, and likely prospect types.
12. Export a professional `.xlsx` with filters, frozen headers, wrapped text, source URLs, match-score color scale, and a `Search Guide` sheet containing the exact Boolean query, rules, data date, coverage caveat, and verification instructions.
13. Inspect key ranges, scan for formula errors, and visually render every worksheet before delivery.

## Required workbook sheets

- `Country Leads`
- `Office Districts`
- `Search Guide`

## Required caveat

Describe the output as a high-coverage public-web prospect list, not every company in the country or an official statutory registry. State that blank fields mean no public detail was verified from the cited source.

## Safety and data quality

- Use only lawfully accessible public or licensed data.
- Cite a source URL per researched company row.
- Do not perform SMTP probing, send outreach, or test phone numbers unless separately authorized.
- Verify current legal entity and contact page before outreach.
- Respect privacy, opt-outs, electronic-marketing, platform, and data-license rules.
